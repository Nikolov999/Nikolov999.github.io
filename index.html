Core Services

Web Application Pentesting

For anything your customers or staff log into — client portals, booking systems, small SaaS tools, admin panels.

I look for broken access control, injection, session issues and business logic flaws that automated scanners consistently miss.

Typical outcome: a clear list of exploitable issues, proof of impact, and concrete changes your developers can action without guesswork.

Network & Infrastructure Testing

External and internal testing to answer one simple question: “If someone targets us, what can they realistically do from the outside, and what happens if they get in?”

I map exposed services, weak configurations, legacy systems and lateral movement paths, then prioritize which ones you should tackle first.

Social Engineering & Phishing Simulations

People are still the easiest way in. I run controlled, authorized phishing and awareness campaigns so you can measure human risk without shaming anyone.

You get statistics, anonymized examples, and concrete training themes instead of vague “users need more awareness” slides.

For exact numbers, the fastest path is a short call: book a discovery call and we’ll scope it properly.

What working together actually looks like

  1. Short discovery call — 20–30 minutes to understand your environment, drivers and deadlines.
  2. Written proposal — clear scope, methods, timelines and fixed pricing. You can share it with internal stakeholders or clients.
  3. Legal & logistics — Scope of Work, Rules of Engagement and Authorization to Test are signed by an authorized party.
  4. Testing window — agreed dates and intensity; I keep you informed about any high-impact findings as they surface.
  5. Report & debrief — you get a full report plus an optional call to walk through findings and next steps.

The goal is simple: no black-box mystery, no drama — just a realistic view of your risk and a path to reduce it.

Why trust me with breaking your systems on purpose?

From home lab work to realistic attack & defense scenarios

I don’t treat pentesting as a checklist exercise. I run full attack chains in labs: gaining an initial foothold, escalating privileges, moving laterally and mapping out complete kill chains — then switching hats and analysing how it all shows up in logs and monitoring.

I’ve also worked through malware and post-exploitation tooling, focusing on how it persists, how it evades basic controls, and which artifacts defenders can realistically rely on.

That background means your test isn’t just “nmap + scanner + PDF”. It’s modeled on how real attacks unfold.

AI-assisted tools I’ve built to support security work

To go beyond manual checks, I build my own tools and automations to speed up analysis and surface the highest-risk areas first:

  • AI Phishing Analyzer — integrates with authorized mail accounts to classify and explain suspicious emails, helping teams understand why something is risky.
  • AI Vulnerability Analyzer — combines service discovery with public CVEs to spotlight the most dangerous exposures, not just the longest list.

These aren’t gimmicks. They help me spend more time validating impact and less time copying and pasting scanner output.

Why I do this

I’ve always been more interested in how systems fail than how they’re supposed to work. Pentesting is the clean version of that obsession: break things in a controlled, legal way so you can fix them before someone else breaks them for real.

If you want a rubber-stamped checkbox report, I’m not your guy. If you want someone to really try to get in — and then help you close those doors — that’s where I fit.

Documentation & legal coverage you can show to anyone

Every engagement comes with the paperwork and structure you need to keep management, clients and insurers comfortable.

Scope of Work (SoW)

  • Defines exactly which systems, apps and ranges are in scope.
  • Outlines what is out of scope to reduce surprises.
  • Documents timelines, objectives and expected deliverables.

Rules of Engagement (RoE)

  • Details allowed techniques, tools and intensity levels.
  • Sets operational safety and change control expectations.
  • Includes emergency contacts and escalation paths.

Authorization to Test

  • Formal written approval from an authorized decision maker.
  • Clarifies that testing is intentional, agreed and legal.
  • Helps protect you if questions are ever raised later.

Final Reporting Package

  • Executive summary for management and external stakeholders.
  • Technical findings with evidence, risk rating and remediation steps.
  • Optional remediation retest and updated report once fixes are in place.
