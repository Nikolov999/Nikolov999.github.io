Pricing & Scoping Guide

Every environment is different. A small brochure website doesn’t need the same depth of testing as a complex, multi-tenant SaaS platform or an internal Active Directory network. This page is designed to give you a transparent overview of how I approach pricing, and what typical ranges look like for common engagement types.

How Pricing Works

I don’t bill by the hour or sell “one size fits all” tests. Instead, pricing is based on:

  • The type of test (web app, API, network, scenario-based, etc.)
  • The size and complexity of the environment in scope
  • How deep the testing needs to go (breadth vs depth)
  • Any specific compliance or reporting requirements
  • Timelines, change-freeze windows and retesting needs

Below are example ranges to help you understand what to expect before we discuss a tailored quote.

These ranges are indicative and may vary depending on your exact environment and requirements. The goal is to give you a realistic feel for the level of investment before we discuss specifics.

What Affects the Final Quote?

Key Scoping Factors

  • Number of applications or systems in scope — more assets mean more testing time.
  • User roles & permissions — role-heavy apps require additional access control testing.
  • Environment complexity — integrations, third-party services, SSO, API layers, etc.
  • Depth of testing — broad coverage vs. deep testing of high-risk components.
  • Production vs. staging — live systems may require stricter change windows.
  • Compliance needs — specific frameworks (ISO 27001, SOC 2, PCI-DSS) can influence scope and reporting.
  • Retest and support — whether a formal retest is required after fixes.

Before any engagement starts, we’ll agree a written scope of work, rules of engagement, and clear testing windows so you know exactly what will be done and when.
